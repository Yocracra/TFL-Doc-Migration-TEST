## 1\. Purpose of This Safety Manual

This document outlines the minimum safety information required to operate the reflow oven in the fab lab. It focuses on:

  * Hazards that can cause injury or damage
  * Required protective measures
  * Emergency response procedures



This document does not teach you how to operate the machine. Refer to the linked Operations Manual for usage instructions.

## 2\. Who May Use This Machine

This machine may be used by:

  * Authorized Fab Lab users and trained student workers



Usage conditions:

  * A trained staff member or authorized supervisor is present in the room
  * User has reviewed this Safety Manual and the linked Operations Manual before use



* * *

## 3\. Primary Hazards

Be aware of the following hazards when using this machine:

  * Thermal - Hot plate, hood, and other external surfaces can cause burns during use and remain hot for a long time after use
  * Electrical - Shock/fire risk if connected to the wrong receptacle, if grounding is questionable, or if cord/plug damage is present
  * Chemical/Fume - Flux fumes and heated PCB materials may produce irritating odors/fumes; overheating can create smoke



Applicable hazard categories for this machine include thermal, electrical, and chemical/fume exposure (from heated PCB materials/flux).

  * Electrical
  * Thermal (hot surfaces)
  * Mechanical (pinch/crush points)
  * Chemical (resins, solvents)



## 4\. Required Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

The following PPE is required when operating this machine or handling materials beyond regular supervision:

  * Protective eyewear (safety glasses or goggles)
  * Heat-resistant gloves (required when hood is open / handling hot boards or tools)
  * Smock or heat-resistant apron (recommended/required during open-hood handling of hot boards)



## 5\. General Safety Rules

Always follow these rules when using this machine:

  * Never bypass safety interlocks or guards
  * Never leave the machine unattended unless explicitly allowed
  * Keep hands, hair, clothing, and loose items clear of moving parts
  * Do not operate the machine if you are unsure how to proceed



## 6\. Emergency Stop & Safety Features

Know the location and function of the following before operating the machine:

  * Emergency Stop: No dedicated emergency-stop button is identified in the provided manuals; use the power rocker switch to shut off the unit immediately
  * Power Disconnect: Front-panel power rocker switch and the wall receptacle/plug supplying the unit (115 V, 20 A per provided manual)
  * Safety Interlocks / Covers: Glass hood provides a heat barrier/cover; no safety interlock is documented in the provided manuals. Do not use if hood/handle/glass is damaged



## 7\. Unsafe Conditions – Stop Immediately If:

Stop using the machine and notify Fab Lab staff immediately if any of the following occur:

  * Unexpected overheating, uncontrolled temperature behavior, or fan not running when convection/reflow mode is intended
  * Strong burning smell, visible smoke (beyond normal flux fumes), sparks, or signs of flame risk
  * Damaged cord/plug, cracked glass, loose components, intermittent power, or any machine malfunction
  * You cannot safely supervise the unit, or you are about to use it outside intended applications/materials



Do not attempt to diagnose or repair major issues yourself.

## 8\. Incident & Injury Response

If an incident occurs:

  1. Stop the machine using the emergency stop if safe to do so
  2. Move to a safe distance
  3. Notify Fab Lab staff immediately
  4. For injuries, staff will follow Fab Lab emergency procedures



* * *

## 9\. Chemical & Material Safety

This machine uses chemicals, resins, solvents, or consumables:

  * Approved PCB assemblies/components and solder paste/flux residues only; leaded solder is prohibited for this machine per the operations manual
  * Allow the unit to cool before cleaning. Wipe surfaces with a soft damp cloth; clean tempered glass only when cool with alcohol or glass cleaner. Dispose of wipes/residue and failed boards per Fab Lab procedures
  * Operate in the designated studio area with normal lab ventilation. If smoke or strong fumes occur, stop use immediately, power off, and notify staff



## 10\. Acknowledgement

By using this machine, you acknowledge that:

  * You have reviewed this Safety Manual
  * You understand the primary hazards and required precautions
  * You will follow all Fab Lab safety rules
  * You will ask staff for help if you are unsure or uncomfortable
