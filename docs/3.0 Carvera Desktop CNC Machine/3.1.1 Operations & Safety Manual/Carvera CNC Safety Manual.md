## 1\. Purpose of This Safety Manual

This document outlines the minimum safety information required to operate the Makera Carvera Desktop CNC Mill at the Fab Lab. It focuses on: 

  * Hazards that can cause injury or damage
  * Required protective measures
  * Emergency response procedures



This document does not teach you how to operate the machine. Refer to the linked Operations Manual for usage instructions.

## 2\. Who May Use This Machine

This machine may be used by:

  * Fab Lab users



Usage conditions:

  * Staff must be present
  * Unusual materials must be approved by staff (see 9\. Approved Materials)
  * Required TAMU laser training
  * Acknowledgment of this safety manual



## 3\. Primary Hazards

Be aware of the following hazards when using this machine:

  * Sharp Objects – Handle both the tools and the cutting materials with caution, as they may have sharp edges. 
  * Optical (Laser) – The CO2 laser can cause severe eye injury if required PPE is not worn.
  * Thermal (Fire & Burns) – Friction from cutting tools can create heat, smoke, and flame. Laser cutting may ignite materials or create a small flame on the surface. Direct skin contact with recently-cut materials and end mills may cause burns. 
  * Mechanical (Moving Parts) – the spindle and laser head move before, during, and after operation. By placing your fingers in the moving system, they may be pinched or crushed. 
  * Electrical – The internal power uses high voltage, along with the main laser tube.
  * Chemical & Air Quality – Friction and laser cutting produces smoke and fumes, which may contain hazardous compounds and chemicals.



## 4\. Required Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

The following PPE is required when operating this machine:

  * (Required) Laser goggles during laser use.
  * (Required) Safety glasses for post processing like sanding, cutting, snapping.
  * For milling, no special PPE is required beyond standard Fab Lab attire.
  * (Recommended) Gloves for handling metal parts with sharp edges after cutting.

## 5\. General Safety Rules

Always follow these rules when using this machine:

  * Never open the lid until the print job is complete.
  * Never bypass safety interlocks or guards.
  * Never operate the machine without the ventilation system running.
  * Never leave the machine unattended. Remain near the EMERGENCY STOP button.
  * Do not touch internal machine components marked DNT (Do Not Touch) in the operation manual.
  * Keep hands, hair, clothing, and loose items clear of moving parts.
  * Do not operate the machine if you are unsure how to proceed.
  * Immediately stop operation if smoke, flame, or abnormal noise/behavior occurs.



## 6\. Emergency Stop & Safety Features

Know the location and function of the following before operating the machine:

  * Emergency Stop:


  * The red “Emergency Stop” on the table next to the machine will stop the machine.
  * Hitting the red “STOP” UI button in the bottom right will pause the operation.
  * If the unsafe situation persists, flick the power button at the rear of the machine.
  * If a flame persists after said above, open the lid and throw the fire blanket on top of the workpiece.


  * Power Disconnect:


  * The ON/OFF switch is at the rear right side of the machine.


  * Safety Interlocks:


  * All functions are locked after an emergency stop, and the machine must be “released” through the controller.
  * The machine will not run without input from the controller.

## 7\. Unsafe Conditions – Stop Immediately If:

Stop using the machine and notify Fab Lab staff immediately if any of the following occur:

  * Flame, especially if persisting longer than a few seconds.
  * Fire does not extinguish automatically.
  * Smoke accumulates inside the machine.
  * Smoke leaks from the machine.
  * Loud grinding, knocking, or any atypical mechanical noise.
  * Strong burning odor not typical for the material.
  * The ventilation system seems to not be operating correctly.



Do not attempt to diagnose or repair major issues yourself.

## 8\. Incident & Injury Response

If an incident occurs:

  1. Stop the machine using the EMERGENCY STOP button.


  1. If necessary, turn the machine OFF by using the rear power switch


  2. Move to a safe distance.
  3. Notify Fab Lab staff immediately.
  4. For injuries, staff will follow Fab Lab emergency procedures.



For fire:

  1. Keep the lid closed if the flames are small and see if they self-extinguish after a few seconds.
  2. Pause the job using the controller or emergency stop.

## 9\. Chemical & Material Safety

Approved materials:

  * Wood


  * Hard and soft woods
  * MDF and HDF
  * (Pending) Composites


  * Metal


  * 6061 Aluminum


  * Plastic


  * ABS 
  * Acrylic
  * (Pending) Hard rubbers



This list is subject to change. Contact a Fab Lab staff member for questions on materials that can be milled.

In general, materials cannot be milled if they are:

  * Brittle and fragile
  * Fibrous
  * “Dead soft”, becoming soft and pliable with heat
