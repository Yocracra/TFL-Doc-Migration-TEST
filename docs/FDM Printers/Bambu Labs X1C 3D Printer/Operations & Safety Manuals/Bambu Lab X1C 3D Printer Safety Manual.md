Bambu X1C 3D Printer Safety Manual

Location: The Fab Lab

Last Updated: 3/12/26

## 1\. Purpose of This Safety Manual

This document outlines the minimum safety information required to operate the Bambu Lab X1 Carbon (X1C) 3D Printer in The Fab Lab. It focuses on:

  * Hazards that can cause injury or damage
  * Required protective measures
  * Emergency response procedures



This document does not teach you how to operate the machine. Refer to the linked Operations Manual for usage instructions.

## 2\. Who May Use This Machine

This machine may be used by:

  * Fab Lab users who have printed one object with staff present



Usage conditions:

  * Printed prior to print without staff assistance 
  * Safety and operation manuals acknowledged
  * Usage of approved materials only



## 3\. Primary Hazards

Be aware of the following hazards when using this machine:

  * Thermal – The printer nozzle may reach up to 300 deg. celsius and the build plate 100 deg. celsius, contact with either when heated for any time may cause burns
  * Mechanical – The printer contains moving axes and belts which may pinch or catch fingers/limbs
  * Electrical – The printer is powered by high current electrical components


  * Fumes / Air Quality – Some printing materials (like ABS) may produce fumes or air particles that could damage the lungs if breathed in



## 4\. Required Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

The following PPE is required when operating this machine:

  * Avoid loose clothing
  * Tie back long hair



## 5\. General Safety Rules

Always follow these rules when using this machine:

  * Never bypass safety interlocks or guards
  * Do not touch the nozzle or build plate while hot
  * Only use approved filament materials
  * Never leave the machine unattended unless explicitly allowed


  * Never leave the printer unattended during the first layer of a print


  * Keep hands, hair, clothing, and loose items clear of moving parts
  * If you smell burning plastic or see smoke, stop the machine immediately
  * Keep the printer door closed during operation
  * Do not operate the machine if you are unsure how to proceed



## 6\. Emergency Stop & Safety Features

Know the location and function of the following before operating the machine:

  * Emergency Stop: Cancel the print from the touchscreen UI
  * Power Disconnect: Rear power switch near the cable, unplug cable if needed
  * Safety Interlocks / Covers: Enclosed printer chamber, automatic print monitoring, thermal runaway protection



## 7\. Unsafe Conditions – Stop Immediately If:

Stop using the machine and notify Prototyping Studio staff immediately if any of the following occur:

  * Unusual noises or grinding sounds occurs
  * Smoke or burning smells appear
  * Printer axes stop moving
  * Filament jams severely inside the extruder
  * Software errors prevent operation



Do not attempt to diagnose or repair major issues yourself.

## 8\. Incident & Injury Response

If an incident occurs:

  1. Stop the print using the pause option if safe to do so
  2. Turn off power if needed
  3. Move to a safe distance
  4. Notify Fab Lab staff immediately
  5. For injuries, staff will follow Prototyping Studio emergency procedures



## 9\. Chemical & Material Safety (If Applicable)

If this machine uses thermoplastic filament materials, including:

  * PLA
  * PETG
  * ABS
  * TPU
  * Other approved/provided filaments



To clean the build plate, the following may be used:

  * Dish Soap & Hot water – for textured build plates
  * Isopropyl Alcohol – for smooth build plates



Safety notes:

  * Only used Fab Lab approved filaments
  * Some filaments may emit fumes
  * Ensure the chamber door is closed for all prints



## 10\. Acknowledgement

By using this machine, you acknowledge that:

  * You have reviewed this Safety Manual
  * You understand the primary hazards and required precautions
  * You will follow all of The Fab Lab safety rules
  * You will ask staff for help if you are unsure or uncomfortable
