## 2\. Maintenance Information

  * Extruder: every 300 hours (~2 weeks with constant use)


  * Check screw tightness
  * Check for left over material and lumps
  * Ensure fans are working properly (check that they spin immediately upon powering on or when the hot end exceeds 50°C, and verify that they are free of debris)


  * Hot End: Every 150 hours (~1 week/whenever necessary)


  * Ensure hot end and nozzle are not clogged (can be simply check by verifying prints are flowing at a normal rate)
  * Clean the nozzle
  * [How to replace a hot end](<https://www.google.com/url?q=https://support.raise3d.com/E2/how-to-replace-the-heating-rods-20-1043.html&sa=D&source=editors&ust=1776804169726040&usg=AOvVaw1sjhuZUkAANrk-7ca6owQA>)


  * Build Plate: Every 150 Hours (~1 week)


  * Clean with isopropyl alcohol to remove residue
  * Check for deformations in the plate (should be done after each print removal ideally)


  * X Guide Rail:


  * Check if print heads move smoothly (~1 week)
  * Lubricate belt (~2 weeks)


  * Both of these can be diagnosed by new strange noises or movements


  * Y Axis:


  * Check screw tightness (~2 weeks)
  * Check build plate moves freely when motors are disabled (~2 weeks)
  * Lubricate belt (~2 weeks)


  * Z Axis:


  * Check screw tightness (~2 weeks)
  * Lubricate axis (~4 weeks)


  * Air Filter:


  * [Replace air filter](<https://www.google.com/url?q=https://support.raise3d.com/E2/how-to-replace-the-air-filter-20-1004.html&sa=D&source=editors&ust=1776804169727338&usg=AOvVaw31dIgBK_STgMDOjLeNDjFn>) (~6 months)


  * See [this page](<https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.raise3d.com/academy/how-to-maintain-the-e2-3d-printer/&sa=D&source=editors&ust=1776804169727564&usg=AOvVaw3W8IaM0bZfCQu11Xxfcj2x>) for more information



## 3\. Swapping Filaments

  1. Go to the utilities screen, select unload on the desired print head
  2. Once the nozzle is heated, press start
  3. Roll the filament roll in reverse until the filament is completely out of the tube
  4. Press done on the screen, then select load
  5. Feed new filament through the tube until you feel resistance
  6. Press start on the screen
  7. Press filament into the tube firmly until you can feel the print head has gripped it
  8. Press stop once the filament is extruding



                -there is still some of the previous filament in the hot end, so stop only once the new filament has begun extruding
