## 1\. Purpose of This Safety Manual[[b]](<#cmnt2>)

This document outlines the minimum safety information required to operate the Glowforge Pro in the Prototyping Studio. It focuses on:

  * Hazards that can cause injury or damage
  * Required protective measures
  * Emergency response procedures



This document does not teach you how to operate the machine. Refer to the linked Operations Manual for usage instructions.

## 2\. Who May Use This Machine

This machine may be used by:

  * Fab Lab users who have approved laser certification[[c]](<#cmnt3>)



Usage conditions:

  * First time usage, staff must be present
  * New material usage, staff must be present & approve
  * Required TAMU laser training
  * Acknowledgment of this safety manual



## 3\. Primary Hazards

Be aware of the following hazards when using this machine:

  * Optical (Laser) – The CO2 laser can cause severe eye injury if the protective lid and other safety measures are bypassed.
  * Thermal (Fire & Burns) – Laser cutting may ignite materials or create a small flame on the surface. Direct contact with skin may also cause burns
  * Mechanical (Moving Parts) – the laser head moves before, during, and after operation. By placing your fingers in the moving system they may be pinched or crushed
  * Electrical – The internal power uses high voltage along with the main laser tube.
  * Chemical & Air Quality – Laser cutting produces smoke and fumes which may contain hazardous compounds and chemicals in inhaled



## 4\. Required Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

The following PPE is required when operating this machine:

  * No special PPE is required beyond standard Prototyping Studio attire.



## 5\. General Safety Rules[[d]](<#cmnt4>)

Always follow these rules when using this machine:

  * Never open the lid until the print job is complete
  * Never bypass safety interlocks or guards
  * Never operate the machine without the ventilation system running
  * Never leave the machine unattended unless explicitly allowed
  * Do not touch internal machine components marked DNT (Do Not Touch) in the operation manual[[e]](<#cmnt5>)
  * Remain nearby during short jobs or when inexperienced
  * Keep hands, hair, clothing, and loose items clear of moving parts
  * Do not operate the machine if you are unsure how to proceed
  * Immediately stop operation if flames persist or abnormal behavior occurs.



## 6\. Emergency Stop & Safety Features[[f]](<#cmnt6>)

Know the location and function of the following before operating the machine:

  * Emergency Stop:


  * Hitting the main UI button in the front/top will pause the operation
  * If unsafe situation persists, flick the power button at the rear of the machine
  * If a flame persists after said above, open the lid and throw the fire blanket on top of the workpiece.


  * Power Disconnect:


  * The ON/OFF switch at the rear of the machine


  * Safety Interlocks:


  * The protective lid interlock prevents operation when the lid is open
  * Machine will not run without job confirmation through the website UI



## 7\. Unsafe Conditions – Stop Immediately If:

Stop using the machine and notify Prototyping Studio staff immediately if any of the following occur:

  * Flame persists longer than a few seconds
  * Fire does not extinguish automatically
  * Smoke accumulates inside the machine
  * Smoke leaks from the machine
  * Loud grinding, knocking, or any untypical mechanical noise
  * Strong burning odor not typical for the material
  * The ventilation system seems to not be operating correctly.



Do not attempt to diagnose or repair major issues yourself.

* * *

## 8\. Incident & Injury Response

If an incident occurs:

  1. Press the main button to pause the job


  1. If necessary, turn the machine OFF by using the rear power switch


  2. Move to a safe distance
  3. Notify Prototyping Studio staff immediately
  4. For injuries, staff will follow Prototyping Studio emergency procedures



For fire:

  1. Keep lid closed if the flames are small and see if they self-extinguish after a few seconds
  2. Press the main button to pause the job


  1. If necessary, turn the machine OFF by using the rear power switch


  3. If they continue, notify a staff member


  1. ONLY PROCEED IF A STAFF MEMBER WOULD NOT BE ABLE TO REACH THE MACHINE IN TIME


  4. Open the lid, throw the fire blanket on top of the material



## 9\. Chemical & Material Safety

Approved materials:

  * Wood
  * Acrylic 
  * Cardboard
  * Rubber (provided)
  * Metal (FOR ENGRAVING ONLY)



## 10\. Acknowledgement

By using this machine, you acknowledge that:

  * You have reviewed this Safety Manual
  * You understand the primary hazards and required precautions
  * You will follow all Prototyping Studio safety rules
  * You will ask staff for help if you are unsure or uncomfortable
